The following variable descriptions are provided by Professor Rohan Alexander:* poll_id: Unique identifier for each poll conducted.* pollster_id: Unique identifier for the polling organization conducting the poll.* pollster: The name of the polling organization that conducted the poll (e.g., YouGov, RMG Research).* sponsor_ids: Unique identifier(s) for the sponsor(s) of the poll, typically organizations that fund the poll.* sponsors: The name of the organization(s) that sponsored the poll (e.g., CBS News, Napolitan News Service).* display_name: The name displayed to represent the pollster, typically the same as the pollster name.* pollster_rating_id: Unique identifier for the pollster¡¯s rating within a rating system.* pollster_rating_name: The name or grade assigned to the pollster by a rating organization (e.g., YouGov, RMG Research).* numeric_grade: A numeric rating given to the pollster to indicate their quality or reliability (e.g., 3.0).* pollscore: A numeric value representing the score or reliability of the pollster in question (e.g., -1.1). "The error and bias we can attribute to a pollster. Negative numbers are better. Stands for "Predictive Optimization of Latent skill Level in Surveys, Considering Overall Record, Empirically.""* methodology: The method used to conduct the poll (e.g., Online Panel).* transparency_score: A score reflecting the pollster¡¯s transparency about their methodology (e.g., 9.0). "A grade for how transparent a pollster is, calculated based on how much information it discloses about its polls and weighted by recency. The highest Transparency Score is 10."* state: The U.S. state where the poll was conducted or focused, if applicable.* start_date: The date the poll began (e.g., 10/8/24).* end_date: The date the poll ended (e.g., 10/11/24).* sponsor_candidate_id: Unique identifier for the candidate sponsored by the sponsoring organization (if applicable).* sponsor_candidate: The name of the candidate sponsored by the sponsoring organization (if applicable).* sponsor_candidate_party: The political party of the candidate sponsored by the sponsor (if applicable).* endorsed_candidate_id: Unique identifier for the candidate endorsed by the polling or sponsoring organization (if applicable).* endorsed_candidate_name: The name of the endorsed candidate (if applicable).* endorsed_candidate_party: The political party of the endorsed candidate (if applicable).* question_id: Unique identifier for the question asked in the poll.* sample_size: The total number of respondents participating in the poll (e.g., 2712).* population: The abbreviated description of the respondent group, typically indicating their voting status (e.g., "lv" for likely voters, "rv" for registered votes).* subpopulation: A subgroup of the population (in this case, left blank).* population_full: The full description of the population, such as "likely voters" (lv).* tracking: Indicates whether the poll is part of a tracking series (e.g., daily or weekly polls). In this case, no tracking is indicated.* created_at: The timestamp when the poll data was created or entered into the system (e.g., 10/13/24 14:23).* notes: Any additional notes or comments related to the poll.* url: A URL linking to an article or report on the poll (e.g., CBS News or Napolitan Institute).* url_article: A URL linking to a detailed article about the poll results.* url_topline: A URL linking to the topline results or summary of the poll (left blank in this case).* url_crosstab: A URL linking to the crosstab data (detailed breakdown) of the poll results (if applicable).* source: The source from where the poll data was derived.* internal: Indicates whether the poll is conducted internally by a campaign or organization (typically marked as FALSE for independent polls).* partisan: Indicates whether the poll has partisan sponsorship or is conducted by a partisan organization (marked as FALSE for non-partisan polls).* race_id: A unique identifier for the political race being polled (e.g., U.S. President).* cycle: The election cycle in which the poll is conducted (e.g., 2024).* office_type: The type of political office being polled (e.g., U.S. President).* seat_number: Number indicating the seat in question (usually blank for U.S. President polls).* seat_name: The name of the seat being contested (if applicable).* election_date: The date of the election the poll is related to (e.g., 11/5/24).* stage: The stage of the election being polled (e.g., general election).* nationwide_batch: Indicates whether the poll is part of a nationwide batch.* ranked_choice_reallocated: Indicates if ranked-choice voting reallocations have been applied in the results.* ranked_choice_round: The round of ranked-choice voting, if applicable.* hypothetical: Indicates whether the poll is about a hypothetical match-up.* party: The political party of the candidate in the poll (e.g., DEM for Democrats, REP for Republicans).* answer: The response or answer choice given in the poll (e.g., the candidate¡¯s party).* candidate_id: Unique identifier for the candidate polled (e.g., 16661 for Kamala Harris).* candidate_name: The name of the candidate in the poll (e.g., Kamala Harris, Donald Trump).* pct: The percentage of the vote or support that the candidate received in the poll (e.g., 51.0 for Kamala Harris, 48.0 for Donald Trump).
